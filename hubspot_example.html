What is an email signature?

An email signature is a professional information block containing your contact details, job title, company logo, and social media links that automatically appears at the end of every email. Is an email signature the same as an electronic signature?

No, email signatures and electronic signatures serve completely different purposes. Email signatures are marketing tools that make emails look professional, while electronic signatures are legal tools that make contracts binding.

Email Signatures vs. Electronic Signatures: 

Email Signatures:

  • Add contact information and branding to your emails
  • Include clickable social media links and CTAs
  • Anyone can create one for free
  • Not legally binding
  • Can be edited or changed anytime

Electronic Signatures:

  • Legally bind you to contracts and documents
  • Function like digital fingerprints
  • Cannot be altered once applied
  • Protect against fraud
  • Require verification software

How to Create A Professional Email Signature

Create a professional email signature by following these five essential steps:

Step 1: Choose a Clean and Simple Design

Select a design that aligns with your professional image and brand guidelines. Professional email signatures use no more than 2-3 colors and stick to standard fonts like Arial, Times New Roman, or Georgia in 10-14pt size. Avoid cluttered layouts that distract from your contact information.

Step 2: Include Your Full Professional Identity

Add your complete name, current job title, and company name to establish credibility immediately. Recipients should know exactly who you are and what you do within one glance at your signature.

Step 3: Provide Essential Contact Information

List your direct phone number, professional email address, and company website URL. Including links to your professional social media profiles, such as LinkedIn, can also enhance your credibility.

Step 4: Personalize with Visual Elements

Consider adding a professional headshot or company logo to make your signature more memorable. Remember to keep the design consistent with your brand and ensure that all the information is up to date.

How do I add my new email signature to Outlook?

To create a signature in Outlook for Windows and Mac:
  1. Log in to your Outlook account and select the Home Tab > New Email
  2. Click the Message tab
  3. In the Include group section, select Signature > Signatures
  4. In the Choose Default Signature section, select the account you would like to associate with the signature from the email account dropdown
  5. In the Select Signature to Edit section, click the New button and name your signature
  6. In the Edit Signature section, paste in your HubSpot generated signature and select OK

  3. Navigate to Email > Compose and Reply
  4. Name your signature in the Edit Signature name field
  5. Paste your signature for Outlook in the Edit Signature section
  6. Select Save

Why doesn't my signature work in Outlook?

When copying and pasting email signatures into Microsoft Outlook, certain formatting may not display correctly if your paste settings are not configured properly. To ensure signatures render with all styles intact, we recommend the following:

For Outlook Desktop (Windows):

  1. In an email message, click File > Options > Mail > Editor Options > Advanced

  2. Scroll to the Cut, copy, and paste section

  3. Set "Pasting from other programs" to "Keep Source Formatting"

  4. Click OK to save changes

  5. Restart Outlook for changes to take effect

Note: These instructions apply to Outlook 2016 and later on Windows. For other versions or platforms, please refer to Microsoft's official Outlook documentation or contact Microsoft support for guidance.

Why doesn't my email signature look as expected?

If your signature doesn't look as expected, here are some solutions to try:

  1. On mobile: Almost every email signature template will display as expected on both mobile and desktop, with the exception of template 3. If you're using template 3, remove any images (headshot and logo) to ensure an optimized mobile display.
  2. Try using a different web browser to create your signature. Some web browsers don't copy the formatting along with the text and this changes with each update of the browsers.
  3. Some email clients require you to copy the source code vs. the email signature itself for uploading. If your signature isn't displaying properly, try copying and uploading the source code instead.

Why does my email signature look different or unreadable in dark mode email clients?

Some email clients automatically invert colors when users enable dark mode, which can make certain signature elements difficult to read. Light-colored text may become invisible against light backgrounds, and some colors may appear distorted.

To ensure your signature remains readable in both light and dark modes:

  • Avoid very light text colors (white, light gray) and choose a darker theme color that will remain visible on both light and dark backgrounds
  • Select a link color that provides good contrast in both modes (dark blues or purples typically work better than bright colors)
  • Test your signature in both light and dark mode email clients
  • Check that your logo displays properly in dark mode—logos with light backgrounds or transparent elements may appear poorly or become invisible

Tip: Dark text on transparent backgrounds and mid-tone brand colors typically provide the best compatibility across different email client themes.
